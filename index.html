About Me

My name is Neshini Sathishkumar and I am currently a senior at Heritage high school. This is my second year in the Independent Study & Mentorship (ISM) program I will continue to study astronomy. To learn more about my topic I have been taking classes such as AP Physics 1 and 2, Astronomy, Earth Space and Science, AP Statistics, and AP Calculus BC all of which will be essential in this field. Outside of ISM II am a member of NHS, H.E.A.T and president of the French club. In general, I’m passionate about learning. I like to paint, read, learn new languages and stargaze. And lastly, through this course, I hope to not only learn more about the opportunities, but be able to pursue them in my field and continue to learn skills that will give me a foundation for my field, and thus allowing me to overcome the challenges I will face.

Mission Statement

My mission in ISM is to further my knowledge in astronomy and gain new skills of professionalism from my peers, teachers and mentors. I will strive to become more confident and acquire new communication skills while in this class. I will learn through my mentor’s experiences and contribute my research to this field. I will continue to stay passionate about my topic no matter how hard it is to learn or how long it will take to reach my goal. So this year, through the opportunities provided in ISM and my own patience and resolution, I will grow my abilities and try my hardest to make sure I can succeed in the future.

About ISM

About ISM

Independent Study Mentorship, or ISM is an exciting, rigorous program designed for high-achieving students. This course allows students to conduct a self-directed, in-depth study of a topic of their choice, while gaining real world experience with the help of a mentor.


Through this program, students develop various skills, such as professionalism, public speaking, stronger communication and interviewing skills. There are also many opportunities available through this class as well, for example, original idea generation, hands on experience in a professional environment and participation in district events.


The ISM program is an immensely beneficial class that allows students to follow their passion and gain invaluable skills along the way to ensure their successful future.

Mentorship

This year, I have had the honor of having Ms.Taylor Hutchison as my mentor. Taylor Hutchison is an astrophysicist and NSF Graduate Fellow at Texas A&M University. She works with some of the most distant galaxies ever discovered, ones which existed when the Universe was barely one billion years old -- meaning their light traveled over 12.7 billion years to reach our telescopes. In order to study these early galaxies, she uses some of the largest telescopes on the Earth (and in space!). Outside of her work and outreach, Taylor places a great deal of importance in the service positions that she holds. She is the Editing Director for #UniqueScientists, an international program dedicated to removing sterotypes about what a scientist "should" look like -- something that is very important to her as a bisexual woman in science. Additionally, she has co-founded a number of mentoring and community-focused groups in her graduate program. Taylor subscribes to the belief that the service she can provide to her local and scientific community is equally as important as the scientific research that she accomplishes. Through her guidance, I was able to finish my original work project, create the website for it through html code, learn how to create and use a GitHub account, and finally learn about python and apply it into four different projects
